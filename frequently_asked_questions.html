Frequently Asked Questions


I want to help out, but I am unable to host somebody. What can I do?

There are many other things that you can do to help!

  • Due to safety concerns and other restrictions, we are only able to accept monetary donation at this moment.
  • Help us spread the word. Tell your friends, tell your co-workers, tell your family, tell everybody you know!

What is the process of signing up to be a host?

These are the steps to take if you would like to volunteer as a host.

  1. Sign up. Click here to go to the sign-up page.
  2. Your information will be sent to a government entity that will preform a background check and a house visit to make sure all the requirements are met. All this is done to ensure the safety of an extremely vulnerable population
  3. Once approved, your information will be posted on this website, visible only to approved social workers.
  4. If a social worker think your space is a fit for a client of theirs, they will contact you via email or text.

Where does my donation go?

Your donation goes solely towards providing for the youth. Part of it is given directly to the host to help them house and feed the youth. We take great care to make sure the money given to the host are use solely on the youth and nothing else.

Why can't I see much on this website?

Since we are dealing with youth, which is an incredibility vulnerable population, we have taken precautions to protect their privacy for their safety and the safety of their hosts. Unfortunately, unless you are an approved social worker or host, you will not be able to see the majority of the profiles of the hosts or the social workers.

What inspired us?

We were inspired by the large amount of Canadians who opened up their homes during the Syrian Refugee Crisis, as well as those who continue to do so. It made us realize that the community, as a housing resource, was not being used to its full potential.

Where are we located?

We are based in Vancouver, British Columbia. Our hosts can be located anywhere in the Lower Mainland.

What is our mission?

Our mission is to connect social workers with volunteer hosts who are willing to open up their homes. This provides a temporary housing option for those with a need to find immediate emergency housing.

Who is eligible to host?

Anybody with the means to can host as long as they pass the appropriate background checks. A private room and a bed is required for the guest.

What does this website do?

This website is a platform to connect willing volunteers in the community with a room to spare and social workers with clients who need a temporary place to live while more permanent housing gets sorted.
